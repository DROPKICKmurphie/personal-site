about.

college daze.

growing up i wanted to be a writer, i loved writing poems, short stories and even most of my assigned papers in class. i always thought i would studdy writing in college, until i wound up in a graphic communications class in 10th grade and began my discovery of graphic design.

i swooned over beautiful visiual design and after completing 3 sememsters in high school at my local vocational school decided to pursue it in college.

i attended columbia college in columbia, sc. a small all-women's liberal arts college. there i studied studio art with an emphasis in graphic design, printmaking, and film photography. i completed a graphic design internship at flock & rally a local marketting and events firm and with goodall gallery the art gallery on campus at columbia college.

post grad.

i was determined to land a job in my field as sooon as possible. i hit the ground running and landed my first full-time design postion at a local screenprinting shop, frenzy tees. there i helped customers with designs for wearable goods. i also was a customer service representative and bookkeeper there.

after a little over a year i began to wonder if i wanted to stick with design throughout my entire career. i loved design but felt like i could expand and do something more. i started looking into frontend development brushing up on my html & css skills through codecademy, free code camp, and skillshare.

i eventually left frenzy and worked a few customer service based jobs serving in resturants, managing north light fibers a micro yarn mill, and as a telemarketer at swype fast, credit card processing company.

currently.

a recent move to asheville, nc aligned things so that i was able to start tech talent south's code immersion bootcamp & now that i'm learning the skills to dive into a career in frontend development, i couldn't be more excited.

when i'm not coding, or designing you can find me knitting hats & socks, reading nonfiction, painting, shooting with my pentax k1000, getting outdoors, traveling, or eating some real good food.

to learn more about my work experience and skills check out my resume, linkedin, github, or behance. get to know more about my personal life by following me on instagram or twitter.
